My goal in the School of Engineering

Engineering is not only about solving problems, but about creating solutions that make the world a better place.
My primary goal in the School of Engineering at AltSchool is to become a well-rounded, skilled software engineer who can build meaningful and scalable solutions to real-world problems. I want to gain not just technical knowledge, but also the confidence and experience needed to work on practical projects whether individually or as part of a team. One of the key reasons I chose the engineering track is because I'm deeply interested in how software is designed, built, and maintained. I want to understand everything from the fundamentals of programming to more advanced concepts like system architecture, data structures, algorithms, and DevOps. My goal is to build a solid technical foundation that will prepare me for complex development tasks in the future. In addition to technical mastery, I want to improve my problem-solving abilities. I aim to approach challenges with creativity and logical thinking, learning to break problems down into manageable parts and finding efficient solutions. I also want to build strong debugging and testing skills to ensure I write clean, reliable, and maintainable code. Another important goal for me is to become more collaborative. I want to learn how to work effectively in teams using tools like Git and GitHub, participate in code reviews, and communicate clearly with other developers. I believe strong teamwork is essential in any tech environment. Long-term, I hope to use the knowledge and skills I gain at AltSchool to contribute to tech projects that make a positive impact in my community and beyond. I also hope to mentor and inspire other young women who want to venture into tech, just like I'm doing now. In summary, my goal is to leave AltSchool not just with certificates, but with the competence, confidence, and connections that will empower me to thrive in the world of software engineering.
